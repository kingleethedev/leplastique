Types of plastic pollution

There are three major forms of plastic that contribute to plastic pollution:

Polystyrene

Polystyrene is commonly found in the environment because it's not biodegradable and it's difficult to recycle. Composed of about 95% air, expanded

Biodegradable plastic

It takes up to 24 months to decompose and can be found in single-use items (cutlery, plates, cups, take away containers).

Polyethylene

It takes more than 450 years to decompose and can be found in detergent bottles, shampoo bottles, outdoor furniture, shower curtains

Places affected by plastic pollution

This areas are affected mostly and how they affected in their daily lifes

ATHI RIVER

Land use in these areas include urban, residential, industrial, transportation, agriculture, and livestock keeping, all contributing to the river's pollution. The Athi River faces increasing clogging, laden with uncollected waste, human sewage, industrial discharges, agrochemicals, and petrochemicals.

DANDORA

Dandora was established in 1977, with partial financing by the World Bank in order to offer a higher standard of housing. The location is in part well known for being the site of the main municipal solid waste dump for Nairobi, which has significant negative health effects on its population.

MOMBASA

Mombasa generates approximately 120 tons of plastic waste every day, of which only five percent is recycled. A significant portion of this waste is dumped into the ocean.
